What is the National Skills Audit

The Government of Zimbabwe has always realised that investing in skills, education and training is critical to developing a sustainable local workforce, facilitating rewarding careers for young people and boosting the country’s competitiveness regionally and internationally. In order to advance industrialisation and modernisation, the Government seeks to address obstacles in the progression from education to employment and entrepreneurship through investing in education and preparation for employment creation in current and future labour markets.

The Government of Zimbabwe therefore wishes to conduct a National Skills Audit (NSA) to identify existing skills, gaps and future skills requirements for the development of a comprehensive empirical strategy that fits with the national vision of industrialisation and modernisation. The results of the survey will enable stakeholders to develop the requisite skills base for industrialisation and modernisation.

Goals

❖ Identify the current skills, future skills, skills gaps, anticipated skills shortages, surpluses, redundancies and emerging skills trends that Zimbabwe has and requires for industrialisation and modernisation.
❖ Establish the relationship between job related competencies and qualifications.
❖ Ascertain the causes of skills shortages by sector and by occupational category.
❖ Identify recent and anticipated technological changes against their implications on skills development.
❖ Determine skills available in the Diaspora that can be harnessed for Zimbabwe’s industrialisation and modernisation.
